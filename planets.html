Planets

Explore our comprehensive guide of the solar system, from inner terrestrial planets to outer gas giants and distant dwarf planets. Discover the unique characteristics that make each celestial body extraordinary.

MERCURE

Mercury is the smallest planet in the solar system and the closest to the Sun. Its surface is heavily cratered and resembles Earth's Moon.

  • Moons: 0
  • Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km
  • Day: 59 Earth days
  • Year: 88 Earth days

VENUS

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and Earth's closest planetary neighbor. It's the hottest planet in our solar system with thick clouds of sulfuric acid.

  • Moons: 0
  • Distance from Sun: 108.2 million km
  • Day: 243 Earth days
  • Year: 225 Earth days

MARS

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system.

  • Moons: 2
  • Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km
  • Day: 24.6 hours
  • Year: 687 Earth days

JUPITER

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. It's a gas giant with colorful clouds and a Great Red Spot—a giant storm larger than Earth.

  • Moons: 95
  • Distance from Sun: 778.5 million km
  • Day: 10 hours
  • Year: 12 Earth years
Read More

SATURN

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and famous for its spectacular ring system. It's a gas giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.

  • Moons:: 146
  • Distance from Sun: 1.4 billion km
  • Day: 10.7 hours
  • Year: 29 Earth years

URANUS

Uranus is an ice giant that rotates on its side. Its unusual tilt makes it appear to roll along its orbital path around the Sun.

  • Moons: 27
  • Distance from Sun: 2.9 billion km
  • Day: 17 hours
  • Year: 84 Earth years

NEPTUNE

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun. It's a dark, cold, and very windy ice giant with supersonic winds.

  • Moons:: 14
  • Distance from Sun: 4.5 billion km
  • Day: 16 hours
  • Year: 165 Earth years

PLUTON

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt. It was once considered the ninth planet but was reclassified in 2006. It has a heart-shaped glacier.

  • Distance from Sun: 5.9 billion km

  • Year: 248 Earth years
