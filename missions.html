Missions

A brief overview of major space missions highlighting their goals and scientific discoveries that expand our understanding of the cosmos and beyond.

APOLLO 11

Agency: NASA

Launch Date: July 16, 1969

The historic mission that landed the first humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above.

Read More

VOYAGER 1

Agency: NASA

Launch Date: September 5, 1977

An interstellar probe that has traveled farther from Earth than any other spacecraft. It carries a golden record with sounds and images from Earth.

Read More

ARTEMIS 1

Agency: NASA

Launch Date: November 16, 2022

The first integrated test of NASA's deep space exploration systems: the Orion spacecraft, Space Launch System rocket, and ground systems.

Read More

HUBBLE SPACE TELESCOPE

Agency: NASA/ESA

Launch Date: April 24, 1990

One of the most successful scientific instruments ever built. It has provided breathtaking images and revolutionary discoveries about the universe.

Read More

OPPORTUNITY ROVER

Agency: NASA

Launch Date: July 7, 2003

A Mars rover that operated for 15 years, far exceeding its planned 90-day mission. It discovered evidence that water once flowed on Mars.

Read More

CURIOSITY ROVER

Agency: NASA

Launch Date: November 26, 2011

A car-sized rover exploring Gale Crater on Mars. It has determined that Mars once had conditions favorable for microbial life.

Read More

ROSETTA

Agency: ESA

Launch Date: March 2, 2004

The first spacecraft to orbit a comet and deploy a lander on its surface. It studied comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko for over two years.

Read More

CASSINI-HUYGENS

Agency: NASA/ESA/ASI

Launch Date: October 15, 1997

A mission to Saturn that revolutionized our understanding of the ringed planet and its moons, particularly Titan and Enceladus.

Read More

JAMES WEBB SPACE TELESCOPE

Agency: NASA/ESA/CSA

Launch Date: December 25, 2021

The most powerful space telescope ever built. It observes the universe in infrared light to see the first galaxies and study exoplanets.

Read More

NEW HORIZONS

Agency: NASA

Launch Date: January 19, 2006

First mission to Pluto and the Kuiper Belt. It provided the first close-up images of Pluto and continues to explore the outer solar system.

Read More
